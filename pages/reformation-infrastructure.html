Reformation and Infrastructure

One of Augustus' Tour 'de Force was the establishment of the Pax Romana, consolidating an era of stability, protection, and prosperity, for over 2 centuries during his reign. Furthermore, Octavian was a mastermind and renowned architect of the Roman Empire; moulding a whole army, formulating civil service, and encapsulating a semantic government structure. Augustus was able to shape an empire from his bare hands, ensuring that his successor, Tiberius, would inherit a successful Rome after Augustus' passing in AD 14.

Army

The army - the centre-piece of the Roman Empire, was divided into 28 legions with fixed wage with terms of service, funded by the aerarium militare. Augustus also established the Prætorian guard for imperial security


Administration

Octavian rationalised tax collection payment for citisens, and contributed to the enhancement of provincial administration by dividing them into Senatorial and military Imperial provinces, forming an efficient and secure civil service.


Moral Legislation

Augustus strongly enacted Lex Iulia laws, which was the backbone for moral and social reformations which were propaganda to revive traditional Roman practices, by pressing indemnities for what was viewed beyond the moral point of view, supporting Augustus' purpose to reform the late Roman Republic into a long-lasting empire.
