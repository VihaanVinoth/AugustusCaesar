Introduction

Augustus Caesar, also known as Gaius Octavius, was the sole founder of the Roman Empire. He was born on the 23rd of September in 63 BC. While Augustus was young, his father was a governor, while his mother Atia was the niece of later emperor Julius Caesar. After Octavian finished his military and academic studies in Apollonia, he claimed his inheritance to rule over Rome, and funded his private army
